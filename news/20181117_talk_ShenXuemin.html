Academician Shen Xuemin, Professor Liang Yingchang and Professor Sumei Sun Give a Talk

2018-11-17

On November 17th, Academician Shen Xuemin of the University of Waterloo (Royal Canadian Academician, Academician of Engineering, Academician of the Academy) came to the laboratory to share with you the future development of the Internet of Vehicles, and aimed at the safety, congestion and environmental protection issues facing transportation An air-to-ground (SGA) integrated network was proposed, which combined cellular, satellite, and drone to achieve scheduling between multiple resources, thereby enabling the connected vehicle to make the fastest, most accurate, and most secure decisions.

At the same time, Mr. Shen also invited two other foreign professors in the same field to communicate with you. One is IEEE Fellow of the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China. Professor Liang shared a new wireless communication technology and proposed a cognitive backscatter network to achieve low power consumption and low cost communication for the Internet of Things.

The other is Sumei Sun, the head of communications and network clusters at the Singapore Institute of Information and Communication. Teacher Sun introduced the performance indicators of 5G networks and existing problems, and proposed a large-scale distributed antenna system (L-DAS) to make 5G more Effectively serve a variety of applications such as the Internet of Things.

In the process of sharing these reports, the laboratory teachers and students were quite aware and understood, and raised many questions related to their own research content.
